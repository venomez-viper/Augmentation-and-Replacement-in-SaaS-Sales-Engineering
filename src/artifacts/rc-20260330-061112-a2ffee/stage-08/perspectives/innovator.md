### Hypothesis 2 — XAI Fluency as a Hard Skill Creates a "Presales Hourglass": Headcount Collapses at the Middle, Expands at the Extremes

**Bold Claim:** AI automation will not uniformly reduce presales headcount — it will create a bimodal distribution: a large low-cost tier of AI-orchestrated demo agents handling commodity POCs, and a small, highly-compensated tier of XAI-literate "technical trust brokers" commanding 2–3× current presales compensation. **Cross-Domain Inspiration:** Labor economics of automation (Autor's task-based framework) + the industrial AI adoption bottleneck documented in ahmed2022artificial. In Industry 4.0, explainability gaps — not capability gaps — are the primary blocker of AI adoption. The same bottleneck structures the presales labor market: the skill that scales slowest under automation (XAI translation for non-technical buyers) is precisely the skill that becomes most valuable.

**Rationale Grounded in Literature Gaps:** Gap 1 identifies that no empirical measurement exists for automation rates across presales subtasks. Gap 2 identifies that no role-transition pathway research exists. Ahmed et al. (2022) establish that XAI is the dominant adoption bottleneck in high-stakes AI deployments. Chang et al. (2024) confirm that risk-level harms from AI are under-measured — meaning enterprise procurement committees will increasingly require human explainability intermediaries that AI tools cannot substitute. The hourglass shape is not predicted in current analyst forecasts (Gartner, Forrester), which model presales displacement as linear.

**Measurable Prediction:** By 2028, compensation data (Levels.fyi, LinkedIn Salary, OTE surveys from SalesHacker/Pavilion) will show: (a) median presales OTE growth <5% YoY for mid-tier roles (3–6 years experience, non-XAI-fluent), and (b) top-decile presales OTE growth >20% YoY for roles explicitly requiring AI/ML explainability skills. The hourglass is falsified if compensation growth is monotonic across experience tiers.

**Failure Condition:** Rejected if compensation data shows uniform growth or decline across all presales experience tiers (no bimodal pattern), or if XAI skill requirements appear in <15% of senior presales job postings by 2027 on LinkedIn/Indeed.

**Estimated Risk Level:** Medium-High — the hourglass prediction is structurally plausible from labor economics and AI adoption literature, but depends on buyer sophistication accelerating faster than AI demo-automation, and on XAI remaining a genuinely scarce skill rather than being absorbed into product UX by vendors themselves.
