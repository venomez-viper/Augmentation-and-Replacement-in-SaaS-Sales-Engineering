# PRISMA 2020: Updated Systematic Review Reporting Guideline

## Cite_Key
page2021prisma

## Problem
The 2009 PRISMA statement needed updating to reflect advances in systematic review methodology and terminology over the past decade.

## Method
Guideline development by an international expert panel; produced a 27-item checklist, abstract checklist, and revised flow diagrams for original and updated reviews.

## Data
Synthesis of methodological advances in systematic review conduct; no primary empirical dataset.

## Metrics
27-item reporting checklist; PRISMA 2020 abstract checklist; revised flow diagrams.

## Findings
Updated guidance covers study identification, selection, appraisal, and synthesis; structure and presentation were modified to facilitate implementation over the 2009 version.

## Limitations
Guideline applicability depends on reviewer adherence; not a methodological standard for conducting reviews, only reporting them.

## Citation
Page et al. (2021). The PRISMA 2020 statement: an updated guideline for reporting systematic reviews. BMJ. https://doi.org/10.1136/bmj.n71
