# Comprehensive Survey on Evaluation of Large Language Models

## Cite_Key
chang2024survey

## Problem
LLMs are rapidly proliferating but their evaluation frameworks are fragmented across tasks, benchmarks, and societal dimensions, making systematic comparison difficult.

## Method
Systematic literature review across three dimensions: what to evaluate (tasks), where to evaluate (benchmarks/environments), and how to evaluate (methods); covers NLP tasks, reasoning, medical, ethics, education, social science, and agent applications.

## Data
Survey of the LLM evaluation literature up to 2024; no new experimental data collected.

## Metrics
Qualitative taxonomy of benchmarks and evaluation methods; summary of LLM success/failure cases across domains.

## Findings
Evaluation should be treated as an essential discipline; current benchmarks often miss societal-level risks; agent and open-ended evaluation remain understudied.

## Limitations
Rapid pace of LLM development means the survey may become outdated quickly; coverage may be incomplete for non-English literature.

## Citation
Chang et al. (2024). A Survey on Evaluation of Large Language Models. ACM Transactions on Intelligent Systems and Technology. https://doi.org/10.1145/3641289
