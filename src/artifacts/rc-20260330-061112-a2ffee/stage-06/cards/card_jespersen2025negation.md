# Negation in English and Other Languages (Critical Edition)

## Cite_Key
jespersen2025negation

## Problem
Jespersen's landmark study of negation across Germanic and Romance languages needed a modern critical edition to remain accessible to contemporary linguistic researchers.

## Method
Historical-comparative linguistic analysis; examination of negative markers, prefixes, double negation, special vs. nexal negation, and negative particles across multiple languages and historical periods; new edition adds Leipzig-style glosses and contemporary numbered examples.

## Data
Historical texts and comparative examples from Germanic and Romance languages.

## Metrics
Qualitative linguistic analysis; no quantitative metrics.

## Findings
Languages exhibit coherent patterns in structuring negative expressions; negative markers undergo cyclical renewal (Jespersen's Cycle); double negation and negative particle diversity reflect systematic cross-linguistic variation.

## Limitations
Primary data drawn mainly from Germanic and Romance languages; limited coverage of non-Indo-European languages.

## Citation
Jespersen et al. (2025). Negation in English and other languages. https://doi.org/10.17169/refubium-47759
