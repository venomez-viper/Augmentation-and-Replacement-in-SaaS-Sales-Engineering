# IoT Forensics: Challenges, Approaches, and Open Issues

## Cite_Key
stoyanova2020survey

## Problem
The proliferation of IoT devices creates rich forensic evidence sources but introduces unique challenges: device diversity, non-standard formats, multi-tenant cloud, jurisdictional issues, and end-to-end encryption.

## Method
Systematic survey of IoT forensics literature; covers theoretical models, privacy-preserving frameworks, blockchain-based evidence integrity, Forensics-as-a-Service (FaaS), data reduction techniques, and forensics readiness strategies.

## Data
Literature review of digital forensics and IoT security publications up to 2020.

## Metrics
Qualitative taxonomy of frameworks, legal challenges, and technical approaches.

## Findings
Chain of custody maintenance is critical and difficult in IoT contexts; blockchain offers promising evidence integrity guarantees; FaaS is emerging as a scalable model; proactive forensics readiness strategies are underutilized.

## Limitations
Rapidly evolving IoT landscape means specific device/platform guidance ages quickly; legal/jurisdictional analysis is jurisdiction-specific.

## Citation
