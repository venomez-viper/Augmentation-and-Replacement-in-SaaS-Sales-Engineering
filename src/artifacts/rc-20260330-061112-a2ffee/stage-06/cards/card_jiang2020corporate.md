# Corporate Governance in China: Agency Problems, Institutions, and SOEs

## Cite_Key
jiang2020corporate

## Problem
China's corporate governance context differs fundamentally from Western markets due to concentrated ownership and horizontal agency conflicts between controlling and minority shareholders, yet is often analyzed using Western frameworks.

## Method
Survey of Chinese corporate governance literature published in top finance/economics journals; covers controlling shareholder agency problems, legal/institutional environment, state-owned enterprise (SOE) governance, and corporate social responsibility.

## Data
Literature synthesis; no new primary empirical data collected.

## Metrics
Qualitative synthesis of empirical findings on governance mechanisms, ownership structure, and institutional factors.

## Findings
Horizontal agency conflict (controlling vs. minority shareholders) dominates over vertical (manager vs. shareholder) conflicts in China; legal and institutional environment is critical for constraining controlling shareholder power; SOEs have unique governance features requiring separate analysis.

## Limitations
Findings primarily derived from listed Chinese firms; may not generalize to unlisted or smaller firms; institutional environment is dynamic and findings may be time-sensitive.

## Citation
Jiang & Kim (2020). Corporate Governance in China: A Survey. European Finance Review. https://doi.org/10.1093/rof/rfaa012
