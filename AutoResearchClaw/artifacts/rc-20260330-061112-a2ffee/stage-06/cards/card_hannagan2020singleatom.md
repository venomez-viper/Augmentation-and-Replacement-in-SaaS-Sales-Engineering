# Single-Atom Alloy Catalysis: Preparation, Mechanisms, and Applications

## Cite_Key
hannagan2020singleatom

## Problem
SAAs offer unique catalytic properties by decoupling reactant dissociation from intermediate binding, potentially breaking transition metal scaling relationships, but a comprehensive review of their mechanisms and applications was lacking.

## Method
Review organized by reaction type; covers SAA preparation (model systems and nanoparticles), surface science characterization, and DFT/experimental mechanistic studies for thermal, electro-, and photo-catalysis.

## Data
Comprehensive literature review of SAA experimental and computational studies up to 2020.

## Metrics
Qualitative and quantitative catalytic performance metrics (selectivity, activity, resistance to coking/CO poisoning) across reaction classes.

## Findings
SAAs break conventional transition metal scaling relations; enable facile reactant dissociation with weak intermediate binding; offer CO-poisoning resistance and coking resistance; reduce precious metal usage while maintaining high activity.

## Limitations
Most fundamental insights from model ultra-high vacuum systems; translation to practical nanoparticle catalysts and reactor conditions remains challenging.

## Citation
Hannagan et al. (2020). Single-Atom Alloy Catalysis. Chemical Reviews. https://doi.org/10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00078
