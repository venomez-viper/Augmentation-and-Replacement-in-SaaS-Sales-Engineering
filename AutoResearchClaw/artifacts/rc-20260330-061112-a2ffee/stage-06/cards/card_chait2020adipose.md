# Adipose Tissue Dysfunction, Inflammation, Diabetes, and Cardiovascular Disease

## Cite_Key
chait2020adipose

## Problem
Obesity-driven adipose tissue dysfunction promotes type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM) and cardiovascular disease (CVD), but the mechanistic links involving depot-specific adipokines and inflammation are incompletely understood.

## Method
Narrative review of adipose tissue biology, focusing on white, brown, and beige depots; analysis of pro-inflammatory secretome, adipokines, lipid metabolism, and insulin resistance mechanisms; covers treatment impacts on adipose function.

## Data
Literature synthesis of adipose biology, metabolic disease, and clinical intervention studies.

## Metrics
Qualitative synthesis of mechanistic and clinical evidence.

## Findings
Dysfunctional adipose tissue creates a pro-inflammatory, hyperlipidemic, insulin-resistant environment; depot-specific adipokine profiles link obesity to both T2DM and CVD; treatment strategies differentially affect adipose function and body composition.

## Limitations
Mechanistic insights largely from animal models; translational gaps remain; inter-individual variation in adipose depot responses not fully characterized.

## Citation
Chait & den Hartigh (2020). Adipose Tissue Distribution, Inflammation and Its Metabolic Consequences, Including Diabetes and Cardiovascular Disease. Frontiers in Cardiovascular Medicine. https://doi.org/10.3389/fcvm.2020.00022
