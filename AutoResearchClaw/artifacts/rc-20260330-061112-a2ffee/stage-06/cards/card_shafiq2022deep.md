# Survey on Deep Residual Networks: Architecture, Performance, and Applications

## Cite_Key
shafiq2022deep

## Problem
Deep Residual Networks (ResNets) substantially improved ImageNet performance, but the reasons for their success and implications for future research were not fully understood.

## Method
Survey reviewing ResNet architecture, residual learning mechanisms, training practices, ImageNet results, and potential extensions beyond image classification.

## Data
Literature review of ResNet variants and benchmarking studies; primarily ImageNet results.

## Metrics
Top-1/Top-5 accuracy on ImageNet; qualitative analysis of architectural design choices.

## Findings
Residual connections address vanishing gradients and enable training of very deep networks; skip connections implicitly create ensemble-like behavior; open questions remain on optimal depth, width, and application to non-vision domains.

## Limitations
Focus on ImageNet may not generalize to other domains; survey scope limited to residual learning rather than broader deep learning landscape.

## Citation
Shafiq & Gu (2022). Deep Residual Learning for Image Recognition: A Survey. Applied Sciences. https://doi.org/10.3390/app12188972
